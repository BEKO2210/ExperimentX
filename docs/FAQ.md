# FAQ

## What is ExperimentX?
ExperimentX is an exploratory, controlled target-identification tool. It helps test whether rank-1 target choices are above chance under documented conditions.

## Does ExperimentX prove paranormal perception or non-local consciousness?
No. It does not provide proof claims. Even statistically interesting results are exploratory and require independent replication.

## What counts as a hit?
A hit is counted only when the actual target is ranked in position 1.

## Why are negative results important?
Negative/null results help estimate realistic effect sizes and reduce publication bias and self-deception.

## Is this a medical or therapeutic tool?
No. ExperimentX is not medical software and should not be used for diagnosis, treatment, or life decisions.

## How should I report results responsibly?
Report total trials, hits, hit rate, chance baseline, binomial p-value, confidence interval, and protocol details (preregistration, exclusion rules, state context).

## Can I compare multiple sessions?
Yes, but only as exploratory summaries. Multi-session patterns are not proof and require stronger preregistered replication.

## What does the integrity hash mean?
The SHA-256 integrity hash checks whether exported session content appears unchanged under the same canonicalization method.

## Is participant privacy handled?
Only local/browser storage is used in V1. Avoid entering personal data in participant codes or notes.
